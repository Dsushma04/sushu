A mental disorder—also called a mental illness or psychiatric condition—is a health condition that affects a person’s thinking, feeling, behavior, or mood in ways that cause significant distress or impair daily functioning. There are many types of mental disorders, including: - Mood disorders: like depression and bipolar disorder, which involve persistent sadness or extreme mood swings. - Anxiety disorders: such as generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, and phobias. - Psychotic disorders: like schizophrenia, which can involve hallucinations or delusions. - Personality disorders: such as borderline or antisocial personality disorder. - Neurodevelopmental disorders: including ADHD and autism spectrum disorder. - Eating disorders: like anorexia nervosa and bulimia. - Substance use disorders: involving addiction to drugs or alcohol. These conditions can be caused by a mix of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors, and they often require a combination of treatments like therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes.
